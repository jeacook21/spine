Another enjoyable read from Erikson. Yet again I was impressed by the scope and scale of his tale. His building of atmosphere was very strong. Rakaru’s aura of menace and intense paranoia came across well.

The parallel and sometimes interwoven plots were enjoyable and the focus on the character development of characters good. The three transformations that Karsa went through: arrogant warrior, quiet guardian to rebel against a good were good narrative devices but I did t get enough sense of his inner life and conflict except that he did not want to be chained. Further we never got a sense (at least in my reading) of Tavore’s motivation. Why did she betray her family. Was it really just naked ambition.

The use of the dead of Rakaru as a deus ex machina was not a plot device I enjoyed. It felt to easy and contrived. The victory to easy. It did however mean that we didn’t get a real sense of Tavore’s worth as a commander. Though we have hints that she expected the Holy desert to support her.

The pacing was good. At over 1,000 pages this is a long book but at no point did I feel as though I would rather be doing something other than enjoying this story.
