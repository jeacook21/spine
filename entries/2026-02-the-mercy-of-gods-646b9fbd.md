What fun. This is a very different premise and world to that of the Expanse. I was frustrated in the first half of the novel finding the cycles of ever worsening suffering bleak and repetative than engaging and enjoyable. In the lattter half I found that the first half was a neccessary crucible for the world and the Corrax that Corey was building.

Dafyd moves through three prisons throughout this novel. The first is the hierarchical world of the academy of his home. He is a lab assistant with unfullfiled ambition, trying and failing not to rely on the patronage of his succesfull and powerful aunt. Then there is the stinking organge hell of the transport ship and finally the "World Palace" of the Corrax. These three prisons represent the three phases of Dafyd's awareness. From disengaged academic to reluctant leader and naisent hope of humanity.

Corey's Corrax are utterly alien, capricious and cruel. But Corey does not fall into the trap of painting them as evil one dimensional monsters. He allows them to have their own motivations and morallity summed up in the phrase "what is, is". The Corrax dominate through strength because that is the nature of the universe, of evolution, of life. How can it be wrong?

I admired the intense psychology of the work. The pressure cooker that is created and the new, at least for me, merger of a prison story and science fiction world. The wider universe and the great confict anre more than hinted at and we are given enough to be curious about what comes next. But the novel can be read as a standalone volume - this I appreciate. It demonstrates Corey's discipline and restraint.

A fitting novel from the authors of "The Expanse". With new ideas and a whole new universe to play in. This is a world I want to return to. High stakes politics, rebellion unwinnable odds. An adventure is in the making here.
