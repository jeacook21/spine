#bookclub

This was a challenging read. It felt like an extended anxiety nightmare. The protagonist is entirely unlikeable. At points I found myself rooting for her if only so her pain would end.

Her predicament is entirely self inflicted she makes a series of horrible judgements and entirely selfish choices justified by what she thinks she feels she deserves.

The novel describes how the publishing industry works, ruthlessly. How it forces authors to compete and how high the stakes are.

Juni implicitly accepts her privilege and sees Asian authors success as a slight against her.

Question the book raises is are all authors magpies steeling the nuggets and content from other peoples lives. Where does inspiration end and theft or plagiarism begin?
