#bookclub

Enjoyed this a great deal. I enjoyed the ambiguity. Is the main character delusional or is he really jumping through time; has he really been abducted by aliens and put in a zoo or is this his psyche’s way of processing the trauma that he has been through in the war.

One of my reflections is that the structure of experiences reflects the situation in the war.

- He is conscripted - he is abducted by aliens

- He is has a forced march through enemy territory held by a group of soldiers who neither want him or care for him - he is carried in a spaceship over which he has no control of his journey. This is also reflected in his time jumps which one could also see as a form of dissociation.

- He is imprisoned in prisoner of war camps - he is held in a zoo by the aliens.

The novel is also a clear anti-war story. The depictions of the cruelty and futility of war are well made. The comparisons between conventional and nuclear weapons are very clearly made irrelevant. It’s not how death and suffering that is produced that is important but its scale. Any death and pain in war is too much, but the scale of Dresden and Nagasaki / Hiroshima are grotesque. The only difference between them is the method of that violation.

The visual imagery was also well done. I felt and smelt the world around me building vivid pictures in my mind. The voice of the narrator was clear. The voices of the other characters distinct.

Though this was a very short novel the scope was impressive. The scale of the plot and pace packed into a short 200 odd pages made the novel feel longer than it was. Not that it was too long, just I finished this novel feeling I had had a long journey that I was reluctant to finish.
