It is hard to write a review of the ninth Malazan Book of The Fallen - "Dust of Dreams" not because it is great or terrible (it is neither) or because I did not like it. But rather it is the penultimate book and I must hold to my faith that Erikson will be able to bring this rambling, incredible, frustrating maze of a story to a satisfying conclusion in the Crippled God.

In the Dust of Dreams Erikson makes his recurring theme of _Compassion_ and his critique on unquestioning loyalty and religion explicit. Compassion is mentioned explicitly 15 times in the novel. It is said rather than explored through the words and actions of the characters. This makes Eriksons message more explicit but weakens the subtle force of his message in _Dust of Dreams_. Elsewhere Erikson has explored compasion through multiple characters such as Coltaine, Itkovian, Fidler and Rake. The powerful example of these characters actions serves the theme far better than simple declaration.

Erikson further enhanced and deepened his critique on human nature. Eriksons exploration of courage was particularly good. His complication of courage marks him as an exception amongst the fantasy writers I have read. Erikson writes of courage as a complex aspect with the possibility of great moral failure.

> Unfaltering courage saw no sacrifice as too great even if that meant leading ten thousand soldiers to their deaths

Even heroism is examined

> No, the true nature of heroisim is a messy thing a confused thing of innumerable sides...
> Many of them ugly and almost all of them terrifying

Erikson links both courage and the notion of heroism to toxic masculinity. The Bagharst are his example. He shows that _courage_ can condemn a society to brutality and an inability to change. The fear the Baghast have of loosing face prevents them from showing compassion to themselves, their tribe mates and the stranger. This ultimately destroys them.

Erikson's treatment of women becomes more vivid and complex and also perhaps more troubling to a casual read. Women, especially the Baghast women, are seen as a group that pull each other down like crabs in a bucket. But Erikson is more subtle. In the hobbling of Hetan he shows that the real source of the women's cruelty is their powerlessness against the abuse they receive from the men. While not excusing this barbaric act and terrible behavior it is explained and the women become another victim of courage. Courage to do the wrong thing.

Later Kisswhere shows a different side of female cynicism when she imagines the meeting of powerful females generals and leaders:

> Eyes like blades - snip slice snip

In my view this is more characature than deepening or refracting the theme of inward self-hatred the women of the Baghast demonstrate. Erikson is at risk of being accused of taking a misogynistic view of women. Though this can be countered by the power and force of the female characters throughout Malazan.

What frustrated me about Dust of Dreams was not the characterization, world-building or pace but the frustration of not knowing or even feeling that we were going anywhere. There is no clear path to an end.

We have had a clash in the wastes, the Bonehunters all but wiped out (apparently) and the rising alliance of humans and K'chain Che'Male. I have never felt the need to take an authors choices on trust more deeply. How on earth Eriskon will resolve all these threads. The use of Icarium as a deus ex machina to solve the invasion of the short tails felt anti-climatic and a waste of this incredible and enigmatic character - it did not resolve a moral choice, nor did it feel as though there was a consequence. Potentially it also means that we may never learn the mystery of Icarium and his lost memories. The birth of the new Azarth in his place gives a clue to a deeper more satisfying purpose but this must be taken on faith. I was hoping for a clearer articulation of stakes, a view of over the horizon to the final act would have helped the reader. But I have learned that Erikson does not hold your hand.

So I look to the _Crippled God_ both with excitement and trepidation. How will Erikson manage to tie the knot. As a reader all I can do is trust that he will.
