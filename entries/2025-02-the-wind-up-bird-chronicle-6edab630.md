#bookclub

This book spancs many genres. Psychological thriller, fantasy, romance and science fiction and mystery. The weaves these styles together to build a character study of Toru Okada. Characters are told very much from Toru's perspective. Or in letters that he recieves from other characters. At many points in the story, especially in Book 2 and most of Book 3 I was convinced that Toru was insane, that he had suffered a psychotic break and his internal world was completely dissociated from the real world. The ending though shows that maybe this was just real.

The book leaves many questions unanswered. Does Noboru Wataya have super-human powers or is his personality and ruthlessness what breaks Kumiko Okada? Are Creta and Malta Kano really psychic or just confidence trixters, did Malta really enter Toru's dream and have sex with him?

I felt the treatments of Malta's defilement or rape by Nororu is interesting. The sense earlier on in the book was that this was all brushed over by Creta from commercial neccesity, a coldness towards her sister. When Malta realy needed Creta she was not there. I felt this may be another way she had betrayed her sister.

The role of May is interesting. The torture of Toru by leaving him down the well and the death of her boyfriend are suggestive as are her letters. Is she just a troubled teenager or is she something more sinister?

I enjoyed the book, never knowing which way the plot would go and what was real and what was not. In some ways it reminded me of Shutter Island by Dennis Lehane.
