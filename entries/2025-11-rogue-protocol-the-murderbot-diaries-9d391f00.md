Finished: 2025-11-19

> An adventure story with a little philosophical seasoning.

Another entertaining story from Wells. The Murderbot is starting to become more and more human. His realisation that Miki was really free where it counted — free to save itself — and therefore that she really was a friend, in the real sense to Don Abdene, felt like a breakthrough. The Murderbot is now questioning everything.

The story follows Wells' pattern in _Artificial Condition_. We start with the Murderbot trying to achieve a goal (in this case gain dirt on GreyCris) but it becomes embroiled with a human group for which he takes on responsibility despite their better judgement. Something is trying to kill them, he does his heroic best to save them and they become grateful and attached. If Wells continues deepening Murderbot's inner life, then I may be able to tolerate the formula, but the balance is fragile.

Miki plays _Artificial Condition’s_ ART in this story. The action was entertaining and some of the larger themes (free will, what is right and wrong, the nature of humanity) play out in the background. The artistic choice to focus on the action and let the development of Murderbot come through slowly is interesting. At first I felt this was simplistic, but on reflection I believe Wells could be doing something interesting here. The Murderbot is going from seeing himself, and the humans it serves, as one dimensional beings. Humans are annoying and idiots, Murderbot kills things. To a more nuanced perspective of the world. His sarcasm is now laced with affection - he acts to protect because he wants to not because he is compelled. He questions what is "nature" _what I'm programmed to do_ vs what is his own free choice.

If each entry in the Murderbot Diaries follows the same plot formula I’m likely to get frustrated and bored. This is why I have given up on Neil Gaiman’s work — too many repeated patterns of character archetype (loner male with magic powers / specialness, kicking back against a remote power).

That said, the novel was entertaining and great fun to read. I will return and see if the plot to _Exit Protocol_ takes things in a new direction. Does Murderbot simply go on an adventure with some humans to protect or is _Exit Protocol_ going to take things in a new direction, certainly away from the rescue mission structure?
