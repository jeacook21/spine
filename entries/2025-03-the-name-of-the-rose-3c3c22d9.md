This was a very different book to the historical novels that I have read in the past.

##### What I liked

I loved the vivid and beautiful descriptions, the world of the Abbey would beautifuly realised, the atmosphere thick and vivid. The characterisation of Adso and Willimam beautifully drawn. The prejudicies and preocupations of people at the time was well explained and the distain that the learned and noble had for "The Simple" was cruel and simplistic, just as I had understood it to be in my limited knowledge of the middle ages. The mystery was allso well done. Even though Jorge was delightfully sinister I didn't suspect him, because of his age and blindness of causing the chatastrophic series of events.

##### What I didn't like

The quotations in Latin. As all the characters in the book would have spoken to each other in Latin, the quotations in Latin which were often not translated were frustrating. It felt like an act of intelectual snobbery by Eco. While it was often possible to infer the meaning of the quotes from context this was not always possible. While not understanding them did not mean that I could not follow the story I felt excluded from a complete understanding of the characters and their lives.
